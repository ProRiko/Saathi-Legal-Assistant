Employment Rights

Handle Resignations & Salary Disputes Confidently

This guide condenses labour-law basics for private sector workers. Use it to plan exits, chase dues, and escalate harassment complaints.

1. Notice Period & Handover

Employment contracts govern the final notice. If your company is silent, rely on Shops & Establishments Acts or Standing Orders. Use the Saathi calculator to benchmark statutory days.

  • Download your contract and confirm probation vs confirmed employee language.
  • Calculate statutory notice using Notice Period Advisor.
  • Document handover tasks in Saathi's Case Tracker to share progress.
Tip: Mention whichever notice duration is shorter between contract and law when negotiating early exits.

2. Unpaid Salary Checklist

Most disputes resolve once HR sees a structured timeline. Assemble documents before triggering a legal notice.

  • Gather salary slips, bank statements, and HR chats for the disputed months.
  • Log escalation attempts with dates in the Case Tracker.
  • Generate the Salary Delay Legal Notice via Saathi notices.
  • Optional: Request manual review inside Tools & Requests for an advocate to validate tone.

3. POSH / Harassment Workflow

Employers with 10+ employees must run an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC). Follow this order:

  1. Write a detailed account in Saathi Chat (anonymous ID stored locally).
  2. Download the Workplace Harassment Complaint template from Agreements.
  3. Mail it to ICC within 3 months of the incident. Escalate to the Local Complaints Committee or police if ignored.

Need legal aid? Use Legal Help Directory to locate NGOs and district cells.

4. Documentation Kit

Saathi cannot enforce payments but helps you stay organised. Maintain:

  • Chronological log in CSV via Case Tracker exports.
  • Scan of ID proof, appointment letter, and proof of work (timesheets, deliverables).
  • Calculator outputs for overtime or maternity dues to justify claim values.
Share the exported bundle with your advocate or labour office to fast-track conciliation.
